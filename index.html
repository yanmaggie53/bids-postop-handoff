A Quality Improvement Study

The Postoperative
Handoff Process

Optimizing how surgical teams transfer patients to the ICU/PACU.

By Maggie Yan

Scroll ↓

The problem

When a patient leaves the operating room for the intensive care unit, a great deal of critical information has to travel with them — what happened during surgery, which medications are running, what the team should watch for next. That transfer of information is called a handoff, and it is one of the most fragile moments in a patient’s care.

Handoffs are often rushed, inconsistent, and dependent on memory. Important details can be lost in the noise of moving a patient, connecting monitors, and managing a fresh post-surgical case.

The goal of this study is to understand the current OR-to-ICU handoff workflow as it actually happens and to find practical ways to make it safer and more consistent.

How I collect data

To understand the workflow, I observe it directly. I sit in on operating rooms at the end of surgeries and take notes on how clinicians work together to prepare a patient for transfer to the ICU.

Then I follow the team as they transport the patient, observing and taking notes the entire way — including how the OR team delivers their handoff report once they arrive in the ICU.

Throughout, I also bring awareness to a postoperative handoff report tool already built into Epic, encouraging the OR team (especially the anesthesiologists) and the ICU clinicians to use it during the handoff.

Findings & Analysis

How I analyze the data

I transcribe my handwritten observations into a spreadsheet, splitting each sentence into its own row so that nothing slips through.

Then I read every row and code it, looking for clinician feedback that falls into one of five categories:

  • Challenges with the workflow or the tool itself
  • Usefulness — where the tool genuinely helps
  • Suggestions for improvement
  • Elements liked about the tool
  • Elements disliked about the tool
